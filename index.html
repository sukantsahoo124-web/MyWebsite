About NavaChar

What is NavaChar?

NavaChar (meaning "New Character / Innovation") is a grassroots entrepreneurship initiative built to ignite the spirit of innovation among school students — especially those from backward and underserved communities.

In a world that's constantly evolving, young minds must move beyond memorizing answers and start asking the right questions — identifying real-world problems and designing meaningful solutions.

We guide students to observe challenges within their communities, think critically, and develop a future-ready entrepreneurial mindset — becoming proactive changemakers, not passive learners.

Our Sessions

What We Teach & How

Each session is hands-on, fun, and deeply practical — turning abstract ideas into real community solutions.

01
🔍

Spotting Real Problems

Students learn to observe their environment with fresh eyes — identifying everyday challenges in their community that others overlook, from sanitation to education gaps.

02
🗺️

Design Thinking Basics

We introduce design thinking in a student-friendly way — empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test. Simple steps for big ideas.

03
🤝

Team Collaboration

Students form teams, assign roles, and work together — building communication, leadership, and teamwork skills along the way.

04
💻

Build a Solution

From idea to action — students prototype their solutions using low-cost materials, sketches, or simple models, making ideas tangible and real.

05
🎤

Present & Pitch

Every student gets to stand up and pitch their idea — building confidence, public speaking, and the ability to communicate a vision clearly.

06
🌱

Community Impact Projects

The best ideas go beyond the classroom. Students are guided to think about how their solutions can create a real positive impact locally.

The Journey

How NavaChar Works

From curious student to confident changemaker — here's your path.

1

📝 Apply & Get Selected

Fill out our simple application form. We look for students who are curious, motivated, and excited to make a difference — no prior experience needed.

2

🧭 Community Observation Walk

Students are guided to walk through their neighborhood and identify 3–5 real problems that affect people daily. This is where every innovation begins — with eyes wide open.

3

💡 Idea Workshop Sessions

Through facilitated workshops, students pick their most impactful problem, brainstorm solutions, and develop their best idea using design thinking tools.

4

🔨 Build Your Solution

Time to get hands-on! Students create a prototype or model of their solution — whether it's a product, service, or campaign. Real ideas become real things.

5

🎤 Demo Day — Present to the World

Students present their solutions in front of peers, mentors, and community members. It's your moment to shine, pitch your idea, and inspire others with your vision.
